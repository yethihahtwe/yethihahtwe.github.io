Executive summary

Objectives

  • To facilitate data collecting and reporting through digitization.
  • To link data reports with a visualization tool.
  • To enhance the IT capabilities of partner organizations.

Outcomes

  • EHSSG has developed a pharmacy inventory application for Android smartphones and tablets.
  • EHSSG and KDHW have begun utilizing a backend database management interface that can integrate to a visualization tool.

Conclusion and Recommendation

Partner organizations find it difficult to adhere to the digitization process due to the fact that people rarely adjust to new situations. EHSSG and the Karen Department of Health and Welfare (KDHW) have begun collecting inventory data from pharmacy warehouses. It is still in the data gathering phase, and once the initial data reaches the cloud database, the visualisation tool will convert it into visually appealing tables, charts, and graphs. This will serve as a model for the remaining organizations, which will thereafter implement the digitization process. Once the pilot programme is successful, more data gathering tools for other areas can be developed and linked to the display output via the backend database. The pharmacy inventory backend database can be viewed in table format, and it is not difficult for EHSSG to manage the cloud database. Other organizations may be unfamiliar with the database backend interface, which could result in the accidental deletion of a particular data set or the entire database. As erased data from a cloud database cannot be recovered, the individuals handling the data may initially require hands-on training though a database user manual was already provided.

Abstract

The goal of the pharmacy inventory data collection project is to establish a foundation for future data gathering and presentation processes. As detailed in previous reports, the data collection app is built on the flutter framework, which can generate native Android, iOS, and Web applications from a single code base. The application is distributed to field data collectors, and app usage training was provided alongside pharmacy management training offered by separate organizations. The database backend interface was created for EHSSG and KDHW.

1.1 Consultant Background

1.2 Data Collection

Data collection is critical for an organization like EHSSG. EHSSG does not collect data because it does not carry out implementation projects but rather strengthens the systems of partner organizations. EHSSG usually struggles to acquire data from partner organizations when it comes to displaying statistics to donor institutions. The Health Information Systems Working Group (HISWG) is capable of collecting data from its partner organizations and ensuring that information reaches the EHSSG. Due to limited resources and present conditions in Burma, implementing groups found it more difficult than ever to collect data and report it to HISWG. The most basic data collector in the current reporting system is capturing images of physical reports and sending them to the head office via an instant messaging app (e.g. Facebook Messenger, Viber, Telegram). The images are delivered to the headquarters, where data entry team manually enters the information into a spreadsheet. Individual organizations keep sensitive information and share the rest with HISWG, which converts the data to a display tool suitable format. The data from each organisation is then aggregated in a Data Display Tool, the output of which is embedded in HISWG.ORG. The EHSSG redirects donor institutions to the HISWG data display.

Image:Data collection process

Some organisations lack the personnel necessary to transform information from photographs to spreadsheets. Some data collectors on the field find it difficult to manage stacks of paperwork. As organisations struggle to manage the reported information, they rarely share it with HISWG, which must wait a considerable period of time to compile the data into a visualisation tool. Sometimes, HISWG receives no information. Consequently, HISWG cannot display the data, and EHSSG cannot guide its donor institutions to data display.

1.3 The Digitizing Process

The objective of EHSSG is to digitize the data collecting and reporting processes. Therefore, it initiated a pilot program to collect pharmacy inventory data. A Pharmacy Inventory Mobile Application is planned. The application captures pharmacy inventory data in real time and works both online and offline. When the user's device is connected to the internet, the information is immediately sent to a cloud database. From the backend, a visualisation tool retrieves information from the cloud database and shows it online, which may then be exported to a specified format.

Image:Digital data collection

EHSSG has begun testing the app by collecting data from its own pharmacy warehouse. KDHW has also started using the app in some of its warehouses. Since a separate cloud database was created and delivered to each individual organization whether it is willing to use the app or not, a particular organization owns its data.

1.5 Firebase

Firebase is a real-time database that stores and syncs data across all connected devices in real time. Firebase is a powerful platform for building mobile and web applications. It provides a rich set of features to help build high-quality apps quickly and easily. Firebase is built on Google’s infrastructure and scales automatically to support growing user base. Firebase also provides powerful authentication and authorization features to help secure the app and protect the users’ data. Firebase is a free and open platform that makes it easy to build, scale, and manage the app. It took a lot of time and effort to create a flutter app, but it is fully customizable, and we can later add other features based on our preferences.

Image:A screenshot of Firebase usage report

I created an initial user interface for collecting pharmacy inventory data. On April 20, 2022, I presented it to organizations (BMA, BPHWT, KDHW, EHSSG) with potential users. I incorporated their suggestions and continued to build the app around their preferences, testing it on smartphones and the web, and releasing it on May 1, 2022. The application is intended for data collection purposes solely. It works mainly for the end-users who are actually entering the data. Data display in the app is particularly for the user’s stock quantity. The backend database is in the form of noSQL data. Since the database is noSQL, there would be no tables. Database admins from ethnic organizations are currently using SQL databases and they are used to handling data in table format. It would be reluctant to use Firebase noSQL database for users getting used to working with tables. However, Firebase is accessible via the Firebase console or a web based client such as "Rowy" which shows the data in table format and exports to Comma Separated Value (CSV). The exported data can be linked to a Data Display Tool such as Google Data Studio. The application framework is sufficiently flexible that EHSSG will be able to give any organization with a unique mobile application and database. Then, five mobile applications with five separate databases will be created for the five organizations that will use the app. EHSSG will provide each organization with owner access to the application framework and database package. Though everything seems to be simple and easy, it would require a usage manual and a usage training for both the application and the backend database.The mobile application works both online and offline. The mobile device should be connected to the internet to utilize the full functionality of the app such as calculations based on input stock quantity. Even if not connected to the internet, data entered by the user is stored locally and persisted even after exiting the app or the device shutdown. Once the device gets online, the app needs to be opened and data is synchronized in the background without asking the user to do anything. The mobile application is fully customizable so that it would be possible to add a user interface with ethnic languages.Some users in the testing phase would like to use the app on Apple devices. Though the app works universally in Android, iOS, Web, Windows and Mac, the apps should be first released through the Apple App Store in order to work on the Apple Devices since these devices don’t allow installing the apps out of the App Store. An Apple Developer account is required and the app is going to be reviewed by the App Store Reviewer Team to get the app verified.

1.6 Rowy

Image:Rowy.io

Rowy is an opensource database management platform which can fetch information from cloud firestore and display in tables. The tables then can be exported to CSV or spreadsheet formats. Database Management Interfaces for EHSSG and KDHW were created since these two organizations have started using the mobile application. Database Management Interface was created using Rowy and customized according to the structure of KDHW's health facility as its hundreds of clinics are also planning to use the mobile application. From this single interface, after logging in, KHDW central warehouse wants to view and edit the entire inventory database of the clinics and warehouses and each district warehouse wants to view and edit the inventory database of its child clinics while excluding data from other non-correlating warehouses. For security reasons, KDHW central doesn't even have a list of its clinics and warehouses and the clinic names kept updated in the database one after another, gradually. Setting up the interface for EHSSG was a lot more easier as EHSSG has only a single warehouse.

Conclusion and Recommendations

Pharmacy Inventory Mobile Application

Partner organizations find it difficult to adhere to the digitization process due to the fact that people rarely adjust to new situations. EHSSG and the Karen Department of Health and Welfare (KDHW) have begun collecting actual inventory data from pharmacy warehouses. This will serve as a model for the remaining organizations, which will thereafter implement the digitization process. Once the pilot programme is successful, more data gathering tools for other areas such as human resource management can be developed and linked to the display output via the backend database. People are used to paperwork and this is what they are comfortable with. Another reason is that certain organizations don't have health workers or clinics confined to a particular area. Most of them are mobile which made it uneasy to work with the records or lists and rather let them function without records and data collection. Moving clinics and health workers are usualy inaccessible to the internet and electricity which is another reason blocking them to switch to digital data collection. Some organizations basically allocate most of their budget to more important tasks rather than investing on phones or tablets. The pharmacy inventory mobile application has been introduced to partner organizations for a few times during meetings. The meeting attendents from a particular organization sometimes don't reflect the opinion of the organizations and they often failed to report to their supervisors or collegues what the meeting was all about. While some organizations have switched to digital data collection, some organizations haven't even heard from their meeting attendents there was an app.
